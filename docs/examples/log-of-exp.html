The logarithm function officially inverts a complex exponential on paper but not numerically:

The graph above is the real part of

\[ -i \log[ \exp( ix ) ] \]

A straightforward inversion says this should be equal to \(x\), but since \(\exp(2 \pi i)=1\) the numerical evaluation is in practice modulus \(2 \pi i\). This is one example of differences to keep in mind between formal mathematics and numerical implementation.
